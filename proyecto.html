How It Works

From Dirty Water to Safe Drinking Water

Our two-phase system first filters physical impurities, then uses solar-heated copper to kill remaining pathogens. Every step relies on gravity, nature, and Sonora's sun.

💧
Phase 1 — Input

Dirty Water Enters

Water from any local source — a well, river, or rainwater — is poured into the top of the filter unit.

1
Phase 1 — Layer 1

Small Stones

The first barrier catches large debris like leaves and dirt clumps, and distributes the water evenly across the filter.

2
Phase 1 — Layer 2

Gravel

Medium-sized particles and sediment get trapped here. The gravel also prevents finer layers below from clogging.

3
Phase 1 — Layer 3

Charcoal (Coal)

Activated charcoal adsorbs dissolved chemicals, pesticides, and organic compounds. It also eliminates bad taste and odor.

4
Phase 1 — Layer 4

Sand

Fine sand traps tiny suspended particles and some microorganisms. A biological layer (biofilm) develops naturally over time.

5
Phase 1 — Layer 5

Cotton

The final physical filter — cotton catches any remaining ultra-fine particles, producing clear water.

🐍
Phase 2 — Solar Pasteurization

The Copper Serpent

Clear water flows through a coiled copper tube inside a glass-covered solar box. The sun heats it past 65°C and the copper kills bacteria on contact. Double protection, zero plastic.

✓
Result

Safe Drinking Water

Water exits clean, pathogen-free, and safe to drink. No electricity, no chemicals, no plastic — just nature and science working together.

Our Innovation

The Copper Serpent

After filtering, water is clear but may still carry invisible bacteria and viruses. The Copper Serpent solves this with a simple concept: a coiled copper tube under glass, heated by the desert sun.

As water spirals through, two things happen simultaneously — heat pasteurizes it and copper's natural antimicrobial properties kill pathogens on contact.

Why Sonliquid

Designed by Students, Built for the Desert

We're a team of students from Sonora who believe that access to clean water shouldn't depend on money, electricity, or imported technology. Our system uses what the desert gives us — sun, stone, sand, and ingenuity.

The Sonliquid system is designed to be built by anyone, with locally available materials, at minimal cost. We're not just building filters — we're building a model that any community can replicate.
